Isaac Swanson

trumpeter

Isaac Swanson is currently the Instructor of Trumpet at Laramie County Community College in Cheyenne Wyoming. While teaching at LCCC, Isaac has built a reputation as having some of the most unique and innovative ways to teaching Lead and Commercial trumpet playing in person and via Skype. He is currently pursuing a Masters of Music in Trumpet Performance and is the Jazz Studies Graduate Assistant at the University of Wyoming where he directs the Jazz III Ensemble and the University of Wyoming Latin Jazz Ensemble. He learned skills from what many would consider some of the best lead trumpet players of our time including Bobby Shew, Roger Ingram, Brian MacDonald, Brad Steinwehe and Wayne Bergeron. In addition to lead trumpet players, Isaac has studied with Bill Pfund (former ITG President), Dr. Robert Murray (formerly of University of Northern Colorado), Dr. Scott Meredith (University of Wyoming), Al Hood (University of Denver), Steven Marx (Colorado State University), John Davis (University of Colorado at Boulder). Isaac attended the University of Northern Colorado were he received a Bachelor of the Arts in Music. Isaac is versatile in many genres of the trumpet including Lead, Jazz, Salsa, Rock, Classical and Baroque Music. He has played with many groups such as the University of Wyoming Jazz Ensemble under the guidance of Dr. Scott Turpen, the University of Northern Colorado Lab I under the guidance of the renowned Gene Aitken, Dana Landry and John Davis and the Aims Community College Jazz Band under the guidance of Dr. Rick Busson. Isaac has shared the stage with Bobby Shew, Wayne Bergeron, Dee Dee Bridgewater, Bill Holman, The Eric Reed Trio, The O'Jays, Tito Puente, Glenn Kostur, Ron Wilkins, Mario Pesacreta, Eric Applegate and Jim White. Isaac has also played with many groups in the Colorado area such as Side Dish, The John Mills Orchestra, Neophonic Jazz Orchestra, Conjunto Colores, Atomic Brass Project, Kream of the Krop, Fort Jazz Big Band, Colorado Swing, Juliana Munoz, La Candela, Kesumba, and George Gray and the Elvis Experience Band. Isaac has performed in a variety of festivals including Mile High Jazz Festival, Yellowstone Jazz Festival, UNC Jazz Festival and the Winter Park Jazz Festival. Isaac has collaborated with various High School and College Big Bands as a performer, director and clinician. He has recorded with the Atomic Brass Project, AJ Salas, The Hornbuckle Brothers, UNC Lab Band I in 1998-1999 with Gene Aitken and in 2008-2009 with Dana Landry.
